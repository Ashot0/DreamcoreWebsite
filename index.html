Marijuana, marijuana - different names for the same plant - cannabis. In America, you can get many different varieties of it, such as: Acapulco Gold, Panamanian Red, Vietnamese Green and New York White. All these names correspond to the strength and place of collection of marijuana. In the US market, marijuana from Mexico and Vietnam is considered the best. Middle Eastern marijuana is also highly valued, but is not readily available. You can't tell which product you're buying without first trying it, as most types of cannabis look alike and smell the same, no matter how strong the exposure. Of all the cannabis varieties, New York white is the most interesting, as it is a plant of great narcotic power, naturally growing in the populous capital. Cannabis is often found in empty car parks, behind alleys and in school yards; but, oddly enough, the place where it often grows is the sewer.

The Department of Health and Sanitation tried to explain this phenomenon in a series of printed reports. They found that the practice of marijuana smokers to throw the seeds down the toilet to avoid arrest led to the development of large underground thickets. The main blame for the numerous blockages and floods in the sewers falls on these thickets. From the reports, it is clear that the sewers have created ideal conditions for the growth of marijuana. It is humid and warm there, and the refuse lying around in abundance serve as good fertilizers. Sewer plants typically reach a height of 12-15 feet and are pale in color due to lack of sunlight. This is the answer to many questions - for example, why rats walk in the middle of Park Avenue.

There are many methods of growing cannabis and each seems to offer the highest yield. I cannot describe all the methods in five books, so I will focus on the two that seem to me the most effective.

Method One Many seeds are fertile, but those from Mexico are the best. Never, under any circumstances, throw away the seeds, because marijuana is a weed and will grow anytime, anywhere.

Growing The first step is to soak the seeds overnight in clean, slightly warm water. Your container can be a regular seedling box. If this is not possible, take a plastic dish about two inches deep, it will do just fine. Fill the container with washed fine sand and crushed sphagnum moss. If this is not possible, use ordinary earth. The earth should be tightly packed, well watered, excess water should be removed. Dig holes half an inch deep along the entire length of the box. You are now ready to plant the seeds. Make holes every inch. Fill each hole with soil, sand, moss and water. Cover the box with a clean sheet of plastic wrap and place it in a warm place with at least six hours of sunshine a day. Leave the seedlings like this until the first sprouts appear. If you do not have the materials described above, the same success can be achieved by placing the seeds in several layers of damp paper towel. Then cover the seeds with a sheet of plastic wrap as above and expose them to the sun.

After about a week, signs of life will begin to appear. In two weeks, individual small sprouts should already grow. Now it's time for the transplant. The field where you want to plant seedlings must be carefully prepared. Manure should be laid at least a week before transplanting. The soil should be similar to the one in the seedling box. Everything else, especially the area of your plot, should be thought out in such a way as to give your plants maximum freedom. The container with seedlings the day before transplanting should be watered so abundantly that the streams of water wash away the weakest plants without damaging the roots. Seedlings should be planted in holes 2 or 3 inches deep, depending on the size of the plants. The soil around the sprouts should be loose, and if you can, add some earthworms to the soil. If there is not enough light, a simple ring of tin foil will help you, with which you should surround the sprout. After planting, the first few days are usually the most critical. If the plants survived this shock, there is no reason why they should not be healthy and tall (15 to 20 feet).

Care At this stage, little care is required, only fertilizer is needed. As a fertilizer, you can use manure, soluble nitrogen-containing compounds, sodium nitrate, ammonium sulfate or humus, popular at all times. To make the plants grow stronger, they should cut off the lower leaves, but this should be done only when the shoots have reached a height of at least three feet. Harvesting

As a rule, shoots should be cut off after they have produced seeds, but if greed rushes you, you can kill the goose that lays golden eggs for you. The best way to dry is in the sun, but if you live in a city where it's not safe to dry 5-10 foot tall marijuana trees, use a quartz or tanning lamp. If drying takes place in the sun, the process takes about two weeks. When using a lamp, the grass is ready for smoking in 3-4 days. After drying, separate the leaves and crush them. This is the best product, of course, if you do not choose to use female marijuana. If so, separate the flowers - this is just what you need. Stems and branches can be used for smoking through a pipe, they can be sold to "friends".

Hemp is a weed and can grow anywhere, including indoors with artificial light. Tanning lamps give a good effect from a distance of 2-3 feet. As an interesting experiment, light half of your plantation with a tanning lamp and the other half with an infrared lamp, then compare the results. For a city dweller, an ideal plantation can be a bathtub or a mixer for making cement (well, it can be smaller).

Second method

This method is somewhat more complicated than the first, but brings truly wonderful results. First of all, you will need a seedling box. It has the following construction: take 1 wooden milk container and cut the sides to a height of 6 inches. Cover the hole with clear plastic, leaving one hole open. Lay a piece of wood across the top of the container and attach a 60 watt light bulb to it. Now you have a seedling box. You will need "Kitty Litter" (you can buy any soil mix) and milorganite (you can replace it with manure). Take one part of manure or milorganite and mix it with five parts of Kitty Litter, fill a box two or three inches full with this mixture, and moisten well. Now spread the seeds, twenty seeds per thirty square inches, on top of the soil and top them half an inch with the Kitty Litter and Mylorganite mixture. Run a 60-watt light bulb 24 hours a day. When the seedlings sprout, turn on the lamp only as an addition to the sunlight.

Seedlings should germinate in a box for about a month, and then they should be transplanted. The landing site should be securely covered, the soil should be well fertilized. When this is done, dig holes about a foot deep and as wide as needed. Give each plant enough space, do not restrict their freedom. To stimulate and help growth, use a variety of fertilizers: humus, milorganite, manure - before planting, as described in method one; after planting, water your plants, add about a glass of slaked lime per square yard of field to the water. It usually takes about eight months for marijuana to go into effect, but it adapts remarkably well to any growth phase. You will always be able to distinguish the female, as her plants are smaller. She needs special care. To preserve the crop, the ideal method is to hang the plants in a well-ventilated barn. Now give the harvest time. If for various reasons you are in a hurry, or there is no suitable shed, you can dry your crop in an oven at temperatures below 200°C. As in the first method, you can use a tanning lamp.

Distribution of marijuana by varieties The flowers of females have the greatest power (these are small shoots and seeds at the very top).
